The IC is dead!
The IC was never more alive!
Everyone's an IC now!

<break time="1s" />

Takes like these on the role of the Individual Contributor float around from time to time. Each sounds right until you hear the next, begging the question - are we in some Sixth Sense moment, where the dead only see what they want to see, not knowing they're already dead? Thoughts on expertise, judgment, and identity keep surfacing. In that spirit, what follows are fragments - short readings from different angles. Not necessarily an argument, but more of a back and forth I'm still having with myself and others.

<break time="2s" />

The dual ladder - an IC track and a management track - was introduced so engineers wouldn't have to become managers. Seventy years later, we automated the engineering and told them to manage the automation.

<break time="2s" />

Sam Altman thanked engineers for writing code "character-by-character." Years of expertise, acknowledged in the past tense. With less tact, one would call it IC as a Service.

<break time="2s" />

Same large language model. 42% success rate on a coding benchmark without scaffolding, 78% with it. The industry calls it harness engineering. An IC just calls it a Tuesday.

<break time="2s" />

December 2025 brought a capability shift in models from Anthropic and OpenAI. Jack Dorsey's response? 4,000 hit the road by spring. The fear of being left behind sometimes moves faster than the proof. Just ask whoever runs Klarna now.

<break time="2s" />

The IC was the person you called when nobody else could figure it out. That was leverage. Now it's a latency problem.

<break time="2s" />

Agent loops made Shopify's templating engine - powering 5 million storefronts - 53% faster. In production. Author: The CEO. The execution barrier disappears for anyone with enough context and taste to steer.

<break time="2s" />

A junior engineer and an unharnessed agent fail the same way: confidently, in the wrong direction, and at an impressive speed.

<break time="2s" />

"Always have an agent running" was how HashiCorp's co-founder put it. That's not an engineering practice. That's a management expectation wearing a hoodie.

<break time="2s" />

"Contributor" - that's now more accessible than ever, which leaves the "Individual" to carry the weight.

<break time="2s" />

The latest Claude Opus model has the lowest sycophancy scores in the industry, at the time of writing. It's still more agreeable than any IC who's been on-call at 3am and knows the spec is wrong.

<break time="2s" />

The IC's mentorship was never a line item. It built careers. Now the only line that matters is the one you type your prompt into.

<break time="2s" />

Two kinds of CEOs right now. One writes a memo: "prove AI can't do it before you hire." The other opens a PR against their own codebase. The distance between those two is the distance between managing the shift and living it.

<break time="2s" />

Everyone's mandating AI adoption. Few are asking who knows how to handle the horse. A harness isn't a boardroom strategy - it's a feel you get from the saddle.

<break time="2s" />

Agency and agent share a root. Latin ah-geh-reh - to act. The former is the capacity to act on your own judgment. The latter acts on someone else's behalf. We gave the IC both and called it progress.

<break time="2s" />

Stripe named their agents "Minions" - a thousand PRs a week, zero human-written code. Anyone who's seen the movie knows what unsupervised minions are capable of doing.
